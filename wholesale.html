Public-sector and contract caterers buy at wholesale, not retail, so this page tracks the same plant-vs-meat comparison at UK foodservice distributors: JJ Foodservice (cash-and-carry and delivered wholesale) and Brakes (contract-catering distribution), whose product prices are publicly visible without an account and scraped daily, plus Bidfood (contract-catering distribution), whose account-gated prices appear only as ratios in a one-day snapshot below. Wholesale method notes

  • Price basis differs from retail and between distributors. JJ Foodservice prices are ex-VAT Collection prices at the Enfield branch (its product pages publish every branch's price; the data records the min-max across branches). Brakes shows an anonymous indicative price based on an "average customer discount": a real caterer's negotiated contract price can differ, so treat Brakes figures as indicative list-level prices, not transaction prices. Bidfood ratios are computed from ex-VAT list prices shown to a new trade account on the snapshot date; the specific prices are withheld because Bidfood's trading terms treat them as confidential. Ratios within a distributor are internally consistent.
  • Catering pack formats: per-100g/100ml figures are computed from each product's stated pack weight or volume (recorded in the register and cross-checked against the distributor's own per-kg unit price where published and where its basis is consistent; the register documents one Brakes bulk-mayonnaise case where the published per-litre figures mix a mass and a volume basis). Pack sizes differ within some pairs; the pair notes flag where that matters.
  • Range gaps are themselves a finding. JJ lists 28 beef burger lines and no beef-style plant burger (its plant burgers are vegetable or coated chicken-style, so the JJ burger pair compares coated chicken formats). Neither JJ nor Brakes lists a vegan coleslaw. At the July 2026 same-brand sweep, JJ carried no Richmond, no vegan Pukka pie and no Magnum; Brakes carried no Richmond and no Ginsters; and no brand sells both cow milk and a plant drink at either. The wholesale plant-based assortment is far thinner than retail.
  • No published wholesale benchmark exists for plant-based products. AHDB publishes weekly GB deadweight (carcase) price series for cattle and pigs, but a carcase price is a farm-gate commodity price, not comparable to a catering product, and no plant-based equivalent series exists anywhere in its taxonomy.
  • Scrape conduct: JJ and Brakes are scraped as anonymous visitors from their server-rendered product pages, within each site's robots.txt (Brakes sets a 10-second crawl delay and a 04:00-08:45 UTC visit window; the daily run is paced and scheduled accordingly). Bidfood is not scraped: its snapshot was curated by hand through a trade account.

Pair matching notes (wholesale)

  • jj-saus (JJ Foodservice, sausages): Branded tier both sides (Quorn vs Blakemans). Meat side replaced 2026-07-22: SGS103 (JJ Butchers 8s, pork 42%) was out of stock at every branch since curation and lost its Collection offers on 2026-07-15. Blakemans Premier is pork (50%), same 8-per-pound (57g) frozen format; pack weight consistent between name (1x4.54kg) and size field (1x80). Larger pack than the plant side (4.54kg vs 2kg; bulk pricing flatters the meat side). Cumberland SGS035 re-rejected 2026-07-22 (weight/count contradiction persists); Tasty Bake 'pork' lines are chicken 24%/pork 14% by ingredients; JJ Quality lines are chicken.
  • jj-mince (JJ Foodservice, mince): Both frozen, own-brand tier. Beef side is 10% fat: JJ lists no 20% fat mince (the retail comparator tier), and leaner mince is more expensive, so this ratio flatters the plant side relative to the retail convention.
  • jj-balls (JJ Foodservice, meatballs): Same 1kg pack both sides, both fully cooked frozen. The beef meatball declares 55% beef content.
  • jj-chickburger (JJ Foodservice, coated chicken burgers): Coated chicken-style burger pair (buttermilk-coated mycoprotein vs battered whole-muscle chicken breast), both fully cooked frozen catering patties. JJ lists no beef-style plant burger against its 28 beef burger lines.
  • jj-mayo-heinz (JJ Foodservice, mayonnaise): Same brand both sides (mirrors the retail Heinz pair) but pack sizes differ (5L vs 10L; larger packs are usually cheaper per litre, which inflates the vegan side's per-litre figure).
  • jj-mayo-sasco (JJ Foodservice, mayonnaise): Same brand both sides; pack sizes differ (5L vs 10L), same caveat as the Heinz pair.
  • jj-mayo-lion (JJ Foodservice, mayonnaise): Same brand both sides (added 2026-07-22). Pack sizes differ strongly (1L vs 2x2.27L; bulk pricing flatters the non-vegan side) and the vegan line is badged Premium while Thick & Creamy is the standard line.
  • jj-sausroll (JJ Foodservice, sausage rolls): Own-label both sides (KaterVeg is JJ Foodservice's plant-based own brand, the same pairing tier as the jj-mince pair; added 2026-07-22). Both frozen 40-count catering packs; roll sizes differ (100g vs 138g), so compare per 100g, not per roll.
  • brakes-saus-chilled (Brakes, sausages): Chilled butcher-style tier both sides (branded premium plant vs 80% pork butcher-style).
  • brakes-saus-frozen (Brakes, sausages): Tier mismatch flagged: branded plant sausage vs own-brand traditional pork in a much larger catering pack (Brakes lists no own-brand plant sausage in a standard frozen format). Similar sausage sizes (50g vs 57g).
  • brakes-mince (Brakes, mince): Both frozen foodservice lines; the beef side is the standard 20% fat tier, matching the retail comparator convention.
  • brakes-balls (Brakes, meatballs): Comparator is pork, not beef (the only beef meatball line, Green Gourmet, no longer renders a live page and appears delisted). Both cooked. The plant side was on promotion at curation (was-price recorded by the scraper).
  • brakes-burger (Brakes, burgers): Comparable patty sizes (109g vs 113g), both frozen fast-food tier (own/foodservice brands both sides).
  • brakes-mayo-hellmanns (Brakes, mayonnaise): Same brand AND same 5L pack size both sides (added 2026-07-22; the cleanest same-brand pair at any distributor). Both amounts are the stated 5L pack volume; Brakes' own published per-litre figures mix a net-mass basis (Real: 22.94/4.695kg) with a volume basis (Vegan: 17.70/5L), so the register cross-check is disabled on both sides and the volume basis is used consistently.
  • brakes-mayo-sysco (Brakes, mayonnaise): Own-label both sides (Sysco Classic is the house range of Brakes' parent company), same 5L pack size (added 2026-07-22; both listed at 15.19 GBP at curation). Cross-check disabled for the same mass-vs-volume unit-price basis reason as the Hellmann's pair.
  • brakes-pie-pukka (Brakes, pies): Same brand AND same variety (chicken and mushroom), both wrapped ready-baked frozen pies (added 2026-07-22). Amounts are Brakes' stated net weights (2.76kg/12 = 230g per vegan pie; 8.03kg/36 = 223g per meat pie); the meat side comes in a 3x larger case, which flatters it on bulk pricing.
  • brakes-ice-magnum (Brakes, ice cream sticks): Same brand and same variant (almond), 20-stick catering boxes both sides (added 2026-07-22; the dairy-vs-vegan stick sizes, 100ml vs 90ml, mirror the retail Magnum pair, so per-100ml is the comparable basis).
  • brakes-cheese-applewood (Brakes, cheese slices): Same brand, smoke-flavour slices both sides (added 2026-07-22). Pack sizes differ strongly (200g vs 750g; bulk pricing flatters the dairy side).
  • bidfood-mince (Bidfood, mince): Both frozen, own/value tier (mirrors the jj-mince pairing). The beef side is 90VL (about 10% fat): Bidfood lists no 20% fat mince, the retail comparator tier, and leaner mince costs more, which flatters the plant side. Branded alternative on file: Meatless Farm plant-based mince. Quorn mince excluded as a bonus-pack promotion (1kg + 50% extra free).
  • bidfood-saus (Bidfood, sausages): Branded plant sausage vs standard fresh pork 8s. Format mismatch flagged: the Quorn side is frozen, the pork side chilled (Bidfood's own-label Everyday Favourites pork 8s pack, 1x80pk, publishes no per-sausage weight, so it could not be used on a per-kg basis).
  • bidfood-burger (Bidfood, burgers): Comparable patty sizes (109g vs 113g), both frozen catering burgers. The beef side declares 70% beef content and comes in a much larger case (5.4kg vs 2kg; bulk pricing flatters it).
  • bidfood-milk-oat (Bidfood, milk vs oat drink): Cheapest per-litre oat line vs cheapest per-litre cow-milk line at the snapshot. Basis mismatch flagged: the oat side is long-life barista formulation in a 6-pack, the milk side fresh in a single 2L bottle.
  • bidfood-mayo-hellmanns (Bidfood, mayonnaise): Same brand and same 5L pack size both sides (the Bidfood twin of the Brakes Hellmann's pair). The non-vegan side is the standard Hellmann's mayonnaise line; Bidfood also lists Light and Professional variants at other prices.
  • bidfood-mayo-heinz (Bidfood, mayonnaise): Same brand both sides (mirrors the JJ Heinz pair) but pack sizes differ (5L vs 10L; larger packs are usually cheaper per litre, which inflates the vegan side's per-litre figure). Bidfood lists no 5L Heinz Original; the 5L Heinz Professional is a different recipe tier.
  • bidfood-cheese-applewood (Bidfood, cheese slices): Same brand, sliced format both sides (the Bidfood twin of the Brakes Applewood pair). Pack formats differ (12x200g case vs a single 750g pack).
  • bidfood-ice-magnum (Bidfood, ice cream sticks): Same brand and same variant (almond), 20-stick catering boxes both sides (the Bidfood twin of the Brakes Magnum pair; the vegan stick is 85ml here vs 90ml at Brakes, so per-100ml is the comparable basis).
