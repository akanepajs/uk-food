Plant-based products vs their meat equivalents: UK supermarket price check

Own-brand and branded pairs at the big UK chains, per 100g/100ml, following and extending the basket in Which? (2022)
Updated daily. Series since 7 Jul 2026; latest prices 7 Jul 2026 (1 day of data). Ratios are averages of daily prices over the series.
Latest picture (7 Jul 2026) The plant-based premium is category-specific. Own-brand plant-based sausages cost more per 100g than pork sausages at every chain with a comparable pair (Co-op 2.1x, Sainsbury's 1.9x, Tesco 1.9x, Waitrose 1.1x), roughly double at most of them. Burgers run 0.4x to 1.2x. Mince and meatballs mostly flip the other way: plant mince is cheaper at all 3 chains with a pair (0.5x to 0.8x vs standard 20% fat beef mince), and plant meatballs run 0.3x to 1.1x. Own-brand vegan mayonnaise carries the largest premium in the basket (2.8x at Sainsbury's), while Heinz sells its vegan and standard mayo at the same price and size.

Wholesale method notes

  • Price basis differs from retail and between distributors. JJ Foodservice prices are ex-VAT Collection prices at the Enfield branch (its product pages publish every branch's price; the data records the min-max across branches). Brakes shows an anonymous indicative price based on an "average customer discount": a real caterer's negotiated contract price can differ, so treat Brakes figures as indicative list-level prices, not transaction prices. Ratios within a distributor are internally consistent.
  • Catering pack formats: per-100g/100ml figures are computed from each product's stated pack weight or volume (recorded in the register and cross-checked against the distributor's own per-kg unit price where published). Pack sizes differ within some pairs; the pair notes flag where that matters.
  • Range gaps are themselves a finding. JJ lists 28 beef burger lines and no beef-style plant burger (its plant burgers are vegetable or coated chicken-style, so the JJ burger pair compares coated chicken formats). Neither distributor lists a vegan coleslaw, and Brakes lists no vegan mayonnaise. The wholesale plant-based assortment is far thinner than retail.
  • No published wholesale benchmark exists for plant-based products. AHDB publishes weekly GB deadweight (carcase) price series for cattle and pigs, but a carcase price is a farm-gate commodity price, not comparable to a catering product, and no plant-based equivalent series exists anywhere in its taxonomy.
  • Scrape conduct: both sites are scraped as anonymous visitors from their server-rendered product pages, within each site's robots.txt (Brakes sets a 10-second crawl delay and a 04:00-08:45 UTC visit window; the daily run is paced and scheduled accordingly).

Pair matching notes (wholesale)

  • jj-saus (JJ Foodservice, sausages): Butcher-style tier both sides. Pack weight from the page's own statement (eight sausages per pound, 64 per box = 8lb). The Cumberland variant SGS035 was rejected: its stated weight (1x3.63kg) contradicts its stated count (1x60 at 6 per pound = 4.54kg). Meat side was out of stock at all branches at curation; its price is still published.
  • jj-mince (JJ Foodservice, mince): Both frozen, own-brand tier. Beef side is 10% fat: JJ lists no 20% fat mince (the retail comparator tier), and leaner mince is dearer, so this ratio flatters the plant side relative to the retail convention.
  • jj-balls (JJ Foodservice, meatballs): Same 1kg pack both sides, both fully cooked frozen. The beef meatball declares 55% beef content.
  • jj-chickburger (JJ Foodservice, coated chicken burgers): Coated chicken-style burger pair (buttermilk-coated mycoprotein vs battered whole-muscle chicken breast), both fully cooked frozen catering patties. JJ lists no beef-style plant burger against its 28 beef burger lines.
  • jj-mayo-heinz (JJ Foodservice, mayonnaise): Same brand both sides (mirrors the retail Heinz pair) but pack sizes differ (5L vs 10L; larger packs are usually cheaper per litre, which inflates the vegan side's per-litre figure).
  • jj-mayo-sasco (JJ Foodservice, mayonnaise): Same brand both sides; pack sizes differ (5L vs 10L), same caveat as the Heinz pair.
  • brakes-saus-chilled (Brakes, sausages): Chilled butcher-style tier both sides (branded premium plant vs 80% pork butcher-style).
  • brakes-saus-frozen (Brakes, sausages): Tier mismatch flagged: branded plant sausage vs own-brand traditional pork in a much larger catering pack (Brakes lists no own-brand plant sausage in a standard frozen format). Similar sausage sizes (50g vs 57g).
  • brakes-mince (Brakes, mince): Both frozen foodservice lines; the beef side is the standard 20% fat tier, matching the retail comparator convention.

Method and caveats

  • Product basket: follows the own-brand and branded pairs in Which? (Dec 2022), "Plant-based alternatives can cost twice as much as meat", re-matched to 2026 assortments and extended with mince, meatballs and additional pairs. Each pair is a plant-based product and its nearest meat/dairy/egg equivalent in the same range tier. The register (with pack sizes and matching decisions) is pairs.json.
  • Prices: scraped daily from trolley.co.uk product pages (a price aggregator; secondary source) because Tesco, Asda, Ocado and Co-op block automated access to their own sites. Sainsbury's rows are fetched from Sainsbury's own product API (primary source) where the product surfaces there; each data row records its source. The scrape respects the aggregator's robots.txt (product pages are allowed; its search is not used).
  • Unit prices: pounds per 100g/100ml computed from the pack price and the register's hand-verified pack weight. The aggregator's own multipack unit prices are wrong (it multiplies pack count by total weight) and are not used, except for two single-container products whose weight the aggregator carries reliably.
  • Shelf prices only: loyalty-card prices (Nectar, Clubcard) and multibuy offers are recorded but excluded from all figures, matching the Which? approach.
  • Averaging: Which? averaged three months of daily prices; this site shows the latest day's prices and averages ratios over the accumulating daily series (1 day so far, since 7 Jul 2026).
  • Coverage gaps: M&S (not on the aggregator; its coleslaw pack weights cannot be verified); Morrisons sausages/burgers/mince/meatballs/coleslaw, Asda sausages, and Co-op mince/meatballs (no comparable own-brand pair found); the 2022 Tesco and Waitrose ready-meal pairs are discontinued.
